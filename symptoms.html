Common clinical features

  • Recurrent acute pancreatitis and severe abdominal pain
  • Nausea, vomiting, and/or fatigue

Physical signs (may include)

  • Eruptive xanthomas
  • Lipemia retinalis

Diagnosis (overview)

In practice, FCS may be suspected with persistent severe hypertriglyceridemia, early onset features, lack of secondary causes, and a fitting clinical picture. Genetic testing can confirm the diagnosis (adapt this to your wording/policy).
